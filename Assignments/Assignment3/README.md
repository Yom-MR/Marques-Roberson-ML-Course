# Clinical Decision Support Systems


Assignment 3: Future Scenarios for AI‑Powered Clinical Decision Support Systems

This essay explored how AI‑powered Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) like Glass Health may evolve over the next 5–10 years. I predicted that future systems will integrate seamlessly with electronic health records, combining large language models with real‑time guidelines, labs, imaging and population data to generate patient‑specific diagnostic suggestions, risk scores and draft care plans. Clinicians will interact via EHR screens, voice commands or short text prompts, while nurses and pharmacists will use the same tools for triage and medication checks.

The paper noted several benefits—earlier disease detection, fewer missed diagnoses, safer prescribing, and reduced documentation burden.  However, it also highlighted challenges: data privacy, cybersecurity, algorithmic bias, and potential over‑reliance on opaque models. Regulatory frameworks and validation standards must mature to ensure equitable and safe use.  Compared to today’s rule‑based alerts, future AI‑CDSS will be far more interactive, context‑aware and generative.
