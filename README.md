# AI in Healthcare Course Portfolio

Hi, I'm Marques Roberson, a college student studying AI and Robotics. I'm interested in AI's capacity to create healthcare solutions because it combines problem-solving, data, and real-world impact in a way that can directly improve patient care. This portfolio documents my coursework, research, and reflections as I learn how AI methods can support clinical decisions, diagnostics, and health equity.

The purpose of this portfolio is to show how my understanding has developed across technical, clinical, and ethical topics. I organized each lab and assignment into its own folder with a notebook and summary so instructors and peers can clearly follow my work.

## Portfolio Overview

- **Lab 1 - AI Glossary:** I built an alphabetical glossary of important AI terms in healthcare and explained each term in practical language.
- **Assignment 1 - Medical Terminology:** I researched and explained complex medical terms, including procedures and biological concepts, to strengthen my medical vocabulary.
- **Assignment 2 - Healthcare Dataset Basics:** I calculated average age and average heart rate from a small dataset and interpreted what those summary values mean.
- **Assignment 3 - Future of AI-CDSS:** I explored how AI-powered Clinical Decision Support Systems might evolve over the next 5 to 10 years, including opportunities and risks.
- **Assignment 4 - fast-RSOM Diagnostic Reflection:** I evaluated a diagnostic test study, explained sensitivity and specificity, and shared my interpretation of the test's usefulness.
- **Assignment 5 - Placeholder:** This folder is intentionally included as a placeholder because no assignment was provided.
- **Assignment 6 - Social Determinants of Health:** I discussed how income, education, and living environment shape health outcomes, and I reflected on ethics, privacy, and fairness concerns.

## Closing Reflection

Through this course, I practiced both foundational technical skills and critical thinking about responsible AI. I learned that successful healthcare AI is not just about model performance; it also depends on clinical context, transparency, ethics, and equitable implementation.
